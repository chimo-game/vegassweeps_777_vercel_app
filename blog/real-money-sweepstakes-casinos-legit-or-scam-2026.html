How the "Sweepstakes" Model Works (Legally)

Unlike traditional casinos where you deposit money to gamble directly, sweepstakes casinos operate under **US Sweepstakes Laws**. This is the same legal framework that McDonald's uses for their "Monopoly" game.

The Two-Currency System

Legitimate sites use a dual-currency system to remain compliant:

  • Gold Coins (GC): Virtual currency used for fun play. It has no monetary value and cannot be redeemed.
  • Sweeps Coins (SC): The "sweepstakes" entries. These are given away for free as a bonus when you buy Gold Coins. **SC can be redeemed for real cash prizes.**

Because you are technically purchasing Gold Coins and receiving Sweeps Coins as a "free gift," you are not "gambling" in the traditional legal sense. This allows platforms like Vegas Sweeps to operate legally in 49 US states (excluding Washington).

Not all sites follow the rules. Here are the major red flags to watch out for in 2026:
